Personal & Education

Born and raised in Richmond, British Columbia, I graduated from the French Immersion program at École R.A. McMath Secondary, where I took an interest in mathematics, science, mandarin, and woodworking. I accepted an athletic scholarship in Track and Field at the University of Toronto, New College. I was an Athletic All-Canadian - taking the silver medal in the 4x400m relay at the 2020 Canadian University (U-Sports) Championships. I'm currently completing the fourth year of my Honor's Bachelor of Science (HBSc.) degree, where I specialize in Economics, Mathematics, and Statistics. My coursework focuses on econometrics, statistical machine learning, and data analysis.

Professional

I have been professionally involved with politics since high school. My first job was as a Constituency Assistant for then-MLA and current BC Liberal Party President, John Yap. I maintained that role for a year and a half in a part-time capacity while attending university. My political experience continued as I supported several campaigns as a volunteer and in paid positions, including the 2019 and 2021 Federal, 2020 BC provincial, and 2022 Ontario provincial elections. Before the 2021 federal writ dropped, I worked in Ottawa for former MP Kenny Chiu in the House of Commons as part of the Conservative Party of Canada's full-time, paid, internship program. Following my stint in parliament, I worked for the Member of Parliament for King-Vaughan, Anna Roberts, as a communications assistant; my work included policy development and speech writing. Included in the Work section are links to the speeches read in the House of Commons.

Data Science

My academic experience centers on statistical and econometric theory, statistical machine learning, regression analysis, and data mining. Complemeting the theoretical elements are hard skills using statistical software and programming, including R, Python, Stata, and SQL. I’ve also learned a bit of HTML, CSS, and JavaScrip on my own to write this website!
